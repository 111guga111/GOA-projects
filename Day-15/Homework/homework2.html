Isaac Newton

Isaac Newton was an English mathematician, physicist, and astronomer. He formulated the laws of motion and universal gravitation which explained planetary motion and objects on Earth. His book Principia Mathematica 1687 laid the foundation for classical mechanics. Newton also contributed to optics he discovered white light is made of colors and mathematics, he co-developed calculus. He built the first practical reflecting telescope which improved astronomy. Later, he worked at the Royal Mint, helping reform Englands currency. His ideas shaped physics until Einsteins relativity.
